About

Hello, I'm Caleb Lanphere, a sophomore computer science at St. Bonaventure University in New York. I have been self-teaching myself programming on and off since I was twelve. Since starting university, I have drastically improved my skills and found the focus I want to spend the rest of my life working towards.

From middle through High School, I had big dreams, but did not have the skills to back up such adventures. I started but failed to finished multiple projects throughout 2018-2024, with two being completed and released. When starting my bachelors program, I gained enough skills to produce complete programs and released my first, from scratch, instant messenger using Java titled TextMe

I am thriving with the structured learning and I plan on releasing more programs throughout my time at St. Bonaventure and throughout the rest of my career.

Relevant Education

User Experience Design | CS-257

Course Description: This course will expose the student to a wide variety of human-computer interaction material to enable them to understand and design positive user experiences with technology. Students will study the theory of user interface/experience design in class and will put that theory into practice through laboratory exercises and teamwork. Ways to learn from users will be covered, as well as design methods including prototyping, heuristics, and best practices. Broader design topics related to current computing trends will also be discussed from a human-centered perspective.

Data Structures and Algorithms | CS-132

Course Description: This is the second course in the computer science major sequence. The programming language Java is used to implement software designs.
